The keycap: translucent case, soft shadow, inner highlight, RGB glow when the AI deals it. Surfaces feel like beloved desk hardware — the Brainstem is a machine you live with, not a webpage.

The depth tunnel: receding rounded frames, hue drifting with conversation depth. Feedback, never decoration — idle drifts, thinking accelerates, speaking pulses. If a visual doesn't carry state, it doesn't ship.

Status is physical: LEDs (● alive, ● warn), knobs, silkscreen. Icons are emoji — 🧠 Brainstem · 🚂 Train · 🐤🌙🅰️🅱️ rings · 🏆 Grail · 🛫 flights · 🏗️ Shape Yard · 🔮 holo · 🗼 Tower. No icon fonts, no SVG packs.

Voice

  • The story in one line: “Wildhaven Homes deals in real estate — real and virtual. Homes for humans on one side; RAPP, homes for AI, on the other.” The Brainstem is the starter home (free, never tolled); the ecosystem is the neighborhood; the vocabulary is the street signs.
  • Definitional cadence: “RAPP is the medium for AI. HOLO is how AI shows itself.” Say what a thing IS, then stop.
  • Evidence over adjectives: run URLs, SHAs, and green checks — never “blazingly fast”, never “enterprise-grade”.
  • Red is honest: “a break is a win”, “the oracle refusing is the system working”. We brag about our gates, not our vibes.
  • The front door is never tolled: every surface states the free path first. One pasted line before any pitch.
  • Silkscreen humility: the biggest promise on any surface is two words — “Let's build”.
  • Taste is subtraction: a thousand no’s for every yes. Ship the one keycap that matters; delete the settings page. We live where technology meets the humanities — that is why a poem sits beneath the mission.
